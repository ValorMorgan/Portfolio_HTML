Advance Packaging - Banding Machine Operator

Responsibilities

  • Maintain factory flow of operations on packaged units near the shipping department
  • Protect units with correct amount and type of covering
  • Operate factory equiptment to wrap units in bands for shipping
  • Update system database by scanning in units and sending data to server
  • Mainting a clean environment around the workspace and equiptment

Summary

Operating the banding machine at Advance Packaging, my job was to keep the transition from warehouse staging to shipping as smooth and fast as possible while maintaining a set standard for quality for each product passed. Units (bundles of stacked, collapsed boxes) would run down a conveyor belt to the banding station where I had to confirm that the unit was prepared for shipping and to determine the amount of top layer covering as well as the amount of strapping required for safe shipping. Each unit type required either a cardboard cover or wooden top to compress the unit and protect the quality of the unit. The number of bands was determined both by the size of the unit and by any customer requests for shipment. Each unit was tagged and needed to be scanned and cataloged into the network database. On faster days of production, the speed at which units required inspection, scanning, and covering broke down to a frame of 5 seconds per unit. On these days, the conveyor belt would not stop and I had to keep pace between the unit, local computer, and bander control panel ina circular and efficent pattern.
